Game Level Design

What the role entails:

When you play games, think about what keeps you wanting to play more. Is it the environment? The way the character interacts with the world around it? Think about what makes a game interesting and exciting to play. This is the job of a level designer. The process begins by sketching ideas out and thinking about how to best approach the level in terms of how the player could act, and all the possibilties of this. The game needs to flow well, with a clear path mapped out but one that is not forcing the player through a narrow path that will inevitably become boring. They need to think about how to get the player to intuitively trigger the events that allows the game to progress, without making it too easy or too obscure.

A level designer should:

  • be able to come up with creative ways of adding missions and challenges to a game.
  • have a good understanding of gameplay mechanics and be able to see all possibilities in terms of what the player could do, even when acting unpredictably.
  • be able to use scripting languages and understand the basics of gameplay programming.
  • have a basic understanding of physics and be able to accurately predict how objects will move within the game when the laws of physics are applied.
  • have developed 2D and 3D design skills and be able to use software such as Maya to create 3D environments.
  • be able to communicate well with other areas of the team such as the artists and design team. They will most likely work alongside the gameplay designers, gameplay testers, as well as the artists and programmers.

So how do I become a level designer?

If you are at school or in college, taking the following courses could be useful in landing you a job as a level designer:

  • Art
  • Art and Design
  • Graphic Design
  • Computer Science
  • Mathematics
  • Physics

Another option is to apply for Level 3 qualifications in one of the following:

  • Games Animation and VFX
  • Graphics
  • Digital Games Design and Development
  • Computing in Art and Design
  • Entertainment Technology: Video Games Art and Design Production
  • Digital Media
  • Creative Media Production & Technology
  • IT:Programming

But what if I've already finished college?

If you've finished college in a different field, then don't fret, you can still develop the skills to break into the level design career.

There are many ways to approach becoming a level designer, but the best place to start would probably be to play games! Think about which type of games you would be interested in creating, and while playing these games, pay attention to the layout of each area and how you as the user interacts with the surrounding environment. Becoming conscious of these things means that you are more likely to get a better understanding of how the level design process works and everything that you need to keep in mind when creating a level.

Buildling a portfolio will be particularly useful as this is where you are able to showcase your work. For most job applications, this will be essential. It is the most optimal way of letting your employer know what you are capable of producing.
